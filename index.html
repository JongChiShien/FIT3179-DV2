Poverty In Malaysia

Trends Over Time

As a developing country, Malaysia naturally still faces some poverty. Over the years, the government has been working to improve its economy to address socioeconomic issues such as poverty. Although poverty has gradually declined, the rates are still not fully satisfactory. Poverty can be measured in three ways: hardcore poverty (extreme), absolute poverty (unable to afford basic needs) and relative poverty (income below average).

Geographic Distribution

The choropleth map highlights poverty across the states with darker shades indicating higher rates. In 2022, national poverty rates were low but some states such as Kelantan and Sabah had rates above 10% which is much higher than the national average. Rural areas are more likely to experience poverty due to limited economic opportunities.

Poverty Factors 1.0

Unemployment is a key factor affecting poverty rates. Individuals without steady income are more likely to struggle to meet basic needs. Higher unemployment reduces access to food, healthcare and education, increasing the risk of falling into or remaining in poverty. In 2009, due to poor economy and lower job opportunities, female are more likely to experience poverty.

Poverty Factors 2.0

Another factor affecting poverty is the financial gap between income and expenditure. This scatter plot shows the relationship between income, expenditure, and financial gap, highlighting households where spending exceeds earnings.
